36 /// Create a 3D Domain consisting of eight rotated Blocks.
37 ///
38 /// \image html eightcubes_rotated_exploded.png
39 ///
40 /// The orientations of the blocks are described in two different ways:
41 ///
42 /// - 1 - As orientations of blocks relative to the physical cartesian axes.
43 ///
44 /// - 2 - As rotations relative to the physical cartesian axes written using
45 /// Rubik's cube rotation notation, for the sake of shorter variable names.
46 ///
47 /// For reference, this is the notation used:
48 ///
49 /// - U - A clockwise 90 degree rotation about the +z axis (The "up" side)
50 ///
51 /// - R - A clockwise 90 degree rotation about the +x axis (The "right" side)
52 ///
53 /// - F - A clockwise 90 degree rotation about the -y axis (The "front" side)
54 ///
55 /// For reference, D, L, and B ("down", "left", and "back") are the inverse
56 /// rotation to the aforementioned ones, respectively.
57 /// Note: Whereas Rubik's cube rotations rotate a layer of the 3x3 puzzle
58 /// cube, we are adopting the notation to apply to rotations of the
59 /// cube itself.
60 ///
61 /// - The -x, -y, -z block has the aligned orientation, that is,
62 /// xi is aligned with x, eta is aligned with y, and zeta with z.
63 ///
64 /// - The +x, -y, -z block has the orientation (zeta, eta, -xi).
65 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation F.
66 ///
67 /// - The -x, +y, -z block has the orientation (xi, zeta, -eta).
68 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation R.
69 ///
70 /// - The +x, +y, -z block has the orientation (zeta, -xi, -eta).
71 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation F
72 /// followed by the rotation R.
73 ///
74 /// - The -x, -y, +z block has the orientation (eta, -xi, zeta).
75 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation U.
76 ///
77 /// - The +x, -y, +z block has the orientation (eta, -zeta, -xi).
78 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation F
79 /// followed by the rotation U.
80 ///
81 /// - The -x, +y, +z block has the orientation (zeta, -xi, -eta).
82 /// It corresponds to the orientation obtained by the rotation R
83 /// followed by the rotation U (equivalently, F followed by R).
84 ///
85 /// - The +x, +y, +z block also has the aligned orientation
86 /// (xi, eta, zeta), relative to the edifice. It is not aligned
87 /// relative to its neighbors.
88 ///
89 /// This DomainCreator is useful for testing code that deals with
90 /// unaligned blocks.
