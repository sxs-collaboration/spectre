36 /*!
37  * \brief Compute the characteristic speeds of NewtonianEuler system
38  *
39  * The principal symbol of the system is diagonalized so that the elements of
40  * the diagonal matrix are the characteristic speeds
41  *
42  * \f{align*}
43  * \lambda_1 &= v_n - c_s,\\
44  * \lambda_{i + 1} &= v_n,\\
45  * \lambda_{\text{Dim} + 2} &= v_n + c_s,
46  * \f}
47  *
48  * where \f$i = 1,...,\text{Dim}\f$,
49  * \f$v_n = n_i v^i\f$ is the velocity projected onto the normal,
50  * and \f$c_s\f$ is the sound speed.
51  */

66 /// @{
67 /*!
68  * \brief Compute the transform matrices between the conserved variables and
69  * the characteristic variables of the NewtonianEuler system.
70  *
71  * Let \f$u\f$ be the conserved (i.e., evolved) variables of the Newtonian Euler
72  * system, and \f$w\f$ the characteristic variables of this system with respect
73  * to a unit normal one form \f$n_i\f$. The function `left_eigenvectors`
74  * computes the matrix \f$\Omega_{L}\f$ corresponding to the transform
75  * \f$w = \Omega_{L} u\f$. The function `right_eigenvectors` computes the matrix
76  * \f$\Omega_{R}\f$ corresponding to the inverse transform
77  * \f$u = \Omega_{R} w\f$. Here the components of \f$u\f$ are ordered as
78  * \f$u = \{\rho, \rho v_x, \rho v_y, \rho v_z, e\}\f$ in 3D, and the components
79  * of \f$w\f$ are ordered by their corresponding eigenvalues
80  * (i.e., characteristic speeds)
81  * \f$\lambda = \{v_n - c_s, v_n, v_n, v_n, v_n + c_s\}\f$. In these
82  * expressions, \f$\rho\f$ is the fluid mass density, \f$v_{x,y,z}\f$ are the
83  * components of the fluid velocity, \f$e\f$ is the total energy density,
84  * \f$v_n\f$ is the component of the velocity along the unit normal \f$n_i\f$,
85  * and \f$c_s\f$ is the sound speed.
86  *
87  * For a short discussion of the characteristic transformation and the matrices
88  * \f$\Omega_{L}\f$ and \f$\Omega_{R}\f$, see \cite Kulikovskii2000 Chapter 3.
89  *
90  * Here we briefly summarize the procedure. With \f$F^x(u)\f$ the Newtonian
91  * Euler flux in direction \f$x\f$, then the flux Jacobian along \f$x\f$ is the
92  * matrix \f$A_x = \partial F^x_{\beta}(u) / \partial u_{\alpha}\f$. The indices
93  * \f$\alpha, \beta\f$ range over the different evolved fields. In higher
94  * dimensions, the flux Jacobian along the unit normal \f$n_i\f$ is
95  * \f$A = n_x A_x + n_y A_y + n_z A_z\f$.
96  * This matrix can be diagonalized as \f$A = \Omega_{R} \Lambda \Omega_{L}\f$.
97  * Here \f$\Lambda = \mathrm{diag}(v_n - c_s, v_n, v_n, v_n, v_n + c_s)\f$
98  * is a diagonal matrix containing the characteristic speeds; \f$\Omega_{R}\f$
99  * is a matrix whose columns are the right eigenvectors of \f$A\f$;
100  * \f$\Omega_{L}\f$ is the inverse of \f$R\f$.
101  */
102 template <size_t Dim>
103 Matrix right_eigenvectors(const tnsr::I<double, Dim>& velocity,
104  const Scalar<double>& sound_speed_squared,
105  const Scalar<double>& specific_enthalpy,
